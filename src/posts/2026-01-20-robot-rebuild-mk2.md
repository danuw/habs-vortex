After weeks of design reviews, prototyping, and a fair few late evenings in the workshop, we are proud to unveil Vortex Mk II — our completely redesigned robot for the second half of the season.

## What Changed

The core drivetrain has been rebuilt from scratch. We moved from a four-motor to a six-motor drive configuration, giving us significantly more pushing power and better stability on the field perimeter. The wheelbase was also widened to lower the centre of gravity and reduce tipping during high-speed turns.

Our intake and scoring mechanism has been redesigned around a new conveyor system that feeds game elements more reliably than the previous claw design. Early testing shows a meaningful improvement in cycle time.

## Autonomous Improvements

Alongside the hardware changes, we have rewritten our autonomous routines from the ground up. Using a combination of encoder feedback and a new gyro-assisted correction loop, Mk II consistently holds its line within a few millimetres over a full 60-second autonomous run.

## What's Next

We are entering Vortex Mk II in its first live competition at the county qualifier next month. The team is putting in focused driver practice sessions every week between now and then. We are confident and excited.
